Review ID: 89932
Submission date: 2026-05-22T19:46:00
Course: COMS W1004 Intro to Computer Science- Programming in Java
Professor: Christian Murphy
Rating: 5
Review text:
I learned a lot about Java in this class, especially as someone whose background was primarily in Python and who knew very little about Java Syntax. I think Professor Murphy is definitely up there among the best professors at Columbia. His policies are very understanding, and he records his lectures and has a very clear, thorough syllabus. Despite teaching some confusing topics, he manages to make them very palpable. No question is ever too stupid, as he does his best to make sure he explains the concept as clearly as possible. On the first day, he explained how he can tell from the question whether someone is just trying to show off their knowledge or really trying to learn, and I really appreciated that. His quizzes aren't hard to study for, as if you just do his practice questions, pay attention/watch his lecture videos, and do the homework yourself, you won't have trouble with them.
Workload notes:
4 quizzes, with the final quiz on the last day of class. 8 homework assignments and reading exercises: the first 2 are worksheets, the next 6 are on Codio, all uploaded to Gradescope, and the reading exercises are on a separate textbook platform that you have to purchase. They're assigned roughly every other week, and there's no homework during the quiz week. The final is 10% of the grade, and he makes it so that if you get questions wrong on the quizzes, you have a chance to redo them on the final to get your grade up, given that those quizzes are 50% of your grade. He has a lot of TAs, so even if you have trouble, you can always check the Google calendar, and there will most likely be a TA or himself who has their office hours scheduled. I would definitely recommend taking Java with him if you can.

Review text:
As someone with little experience in Java, this class was definitely difficult. I would only take this course if you are seriously interested in majoring/minoring in CS - it is not just an elective you can push off to the side. I took COMS1004 to see if I wanted to major in CS and realized it wasn't for me, so the last couple of months were a pain to get through because the homework gets harder and harder. Honesty, everything after the midterm dramatically increased in difficulty. I also found the homework to be inordinately difficult compared to the exams. Expect to spend 5-10 hours on each homework, probably more on the Poker assignment.
Blaer is a very charismatic and enjoyable lecturer. However, if you are someone who doesn't know what is going on, it's easy to get confused because he incorporates jargon that we learned in class into his explanations. If you don't understand a previous concept, MAKE SURE you figure it out asap.
As someone who got an A and was incredibly confused half of the semester, my best advice is to 1) Start HW early and go to office hours for help. There are lots of TAs so I'd suggest going to different ones and figuring out which TAs you like; 2) Actually read the Zybooks and make sure you know how to do all of the problems. For greater understanding, do the challenge problems; 3) Go to the review sessions and study everything he says for the midterm/final. I found YouTube and previous lectures/HWs to be helpful. Final and midterm are similarly structured, and if you're confused start with memorizing the syntax of the algorithms he covers (binary search, linear search, max/min, selection/insertion sort).
I took ESP and that was a super chill, surface-level introduction to CS concepts.
Workload notes:
40% Homework, 10% Zybooks participation (only need to do 80% to get full credit), 20% Midterm, 30% Final. The raw average for my semester was an 82, and he curves the average to a low B+ and the median to an A-, so it is pretty fair.

Overall positive experience. Professor Blaer is a very kind person. He is the most understanding professor of all the professors I have taken class with. He truly tries his best to adjust the release date and homework deadline to make our lives easier because he understands we have a lot of work to do.
He and the TAs are also very supportive, super quick in responding questions on ed.
I have to however agree with other comments that I don't love his teaching style. He is not bad at explaining concepts, but there are no slides or complementary notes for most coding lectures and therefore the learning process feels unstructured. The strategy is to make your own notes with the recordings after class, because it's hard to make a structured notes in class when you don't know where he's going. I however did not realize this early on and did not make my own notes. So although I ended up an A I feel like I did not have a clear picture of what I have learned.
It's a bit ironic because the logistics stuff in this class are pretty structured and organized in my opinion.
I personally did not encounter any difficulties in seeking support, so I disagree with other comments saying he is unapproachable.
Workload notes:
10 homework assignments (40%), weekly Zybook exercises (10%), a midterm exam (20%), and a final exam (30%)
Zybook is basically reading assignments with coding exercises.
Average, maybe a bit heavier workload if you have no coding experience.
Grading is fair. The written responses for both homework and exams, however, are strict in a way that you have to answer exactly what they are expecting. I got points deducted because the response not 100% the same with the rubric, although I already show clear understanding to the question.
